# AskU — Anti-abuse Rules

## Goals
- Prevent spam and flooding.
- Reduce harassment escalation.
- Keep anonymous posting safe and usable.
- Ensure moderators can act quickly on serious violations.

## What is strictly forbidden
The following actions may result in immediate account deletion:

1. **Threats and harassment** — threatening language, targeted insults, or intimidation directed at any person.
2. **Doxxing** — revealing or attempting to reveal anyone's real name, email, student ID, or personal information without consent.
3. **Illegal content** — anything that violates Estonian law or University of Tartu regulations.
4. **Impersonation** — pretending to be another student, lecturer, or moderator.
5. **Repeated abuse after warnings** — continuing banned behavior after a moderator has acted on it.
6. **Spam and advertising** — posting irrelevant content, links, or promotions unrelated to coursework.

## What happens when you break rules

| Severity | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Minor (spam, off-topic flooding) | Content hidden; no further action |
| Moderate (harassment, repeated spam) | Content deleted; account reviewed |
| Severe (threats, doxxing, illegal content) | Content deleted + account permanently removed |

Moderators review all reported content. Every moderation action is logged with a timestamp.

## Rate limits (automatic, server-side)
These limits apply to all users to prevent flooding:

### Questions (threads)
- Max 3 per hour per user (per course).

### Answers (comments)
- Max 10 per hour per user (per course).

### Reports
- Max 5 reports per hour per user.

### New accounts (first 24 hours)
- Threads: max 1 per hour, max 3 per day.
- Comments: max 5 per hour, max 20 per day.

When a limit is reached, the action is blocked and you will see an error message. Limits reset automatically.

## Auto-hide threshold
If a thread or comment receives 3 or more reports from different students in the same course, it is automatically hidden and sent to the moderation queue for review. This does not mean the content is deleted — it is reviewed by a moderator before any final action.

## Reporting content
Use the Report button (flag icon) on any thread or comment. Choose:
- **Spam** — irrelevant, repetitive, or advertising content.
- **Abuse** — harassment, threats, doxxing, or other harmful content.

Moderators review all open reports. False reporting (mass-reporting to silence someone) is itself a rule violation.

## Anonymity and de-anonymization
AskU uses verified anonymity: you are known to the system, but not to other students (unless you choose to show your real name). In exceptional cases (threats, illegal content, repeated severe abuse), a moderator may look up the real email behind a pseudonym. This action is always logged in a tamper-evident audit trail.

## Notes
- All rules apply equally to all users regardless of their display mode (anonymous or real name).
- Numbers (rate limits) may be adjusted based on pilot study feedback.
- Questions about the rules? Contact the platform administrator.
